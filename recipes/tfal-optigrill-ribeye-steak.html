T-fal Optigrill Rib Eye Steaks with Chimichurri Sauce

Description

No doubt about it, grilling was invented to cook tender cuts of meat. Now a days, beef has replaced the leg of wild boar or mammoth steak our ancestors used to feast on, but the pleasure remains the same: everyone loves to sink their teeth into a well-seared, tender and juicy cut of meat with grill marks on the surface.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp (30 ml) red wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp (15 ml) fresh lemon juice
  • 4 garlic cloves, very finely chopped
  • 1 tsp (5 ml) each salt and pepper, divided
  • 1/2 tsp (2 ml) hot pepper flakes
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 3 tbsp (45 ml) olive oil
  • 1/2 cup (125 ml) very finely chopped fresh parsley leaves
  • 2 rib eye steaks, about 175 g each
